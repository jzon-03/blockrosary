SPIRITUAL COMMUNION

Leader:
Let us be bonded together in the grace of Spiritual Communion.
ALL:
Lord, I am heartily sorry for my sins. With Thy divine aid, I promise not to sin and come to any occasion of sin. My Jesus, I believe that Thou art truly present in the Blessed Sacrament. I love Thee above all things, and I desire to receive Thee in my soul. Since I cannot now receive Thee sacramentally, please come to my heart at least spiritually. (PAUSE) I now embrace Thee as already being there, and I unite myself entirely to Thee. Never allow me to be separated from Thee. Amen.

PRAYER INTENTIONS

ALL: God our Father, we praise and glorify you. We thank you for the grace to respond to your call to gather in prayer today. We are here to offer you our past, present and future sufferings with those of Jesus on the Cross in the unity of the Holy Spirit. We are here to pray for Mary’s intentions in Fatima for the conversion of sinners and salvation of souls, world peace and reparation of the sins of the world.

We ask Saint Joseph to walk with us through the mysteries of the Holy Rosary of his Blessed spouse, to intercede for the protection of the Catholic church, Pope Francis, Bishop Matano, the clergy and religious. We repeat Jesus prayer in Gethesemane for Christian unity so that there will be one single flock under one single shepherd. May all fallen away Catholics return to the one true faith. We pray for those who persecute and try to destroy the Catholic church from within and without and for persecuted Catholics all over the world.

May there be an increase of vocation in the priesthood and the consecrated religious life and abundant holiness in those we already have. Guide and enlighten all government officials in the United States and the Philippines to use Christian principles and values in their decision making so that they may foster peace and prosperity in these countries and in the world.

We pray that the poor, the underprivileged and the marginalized will find security and comfort through the generosity of others. May your justice prevail on earth for them. Please guard the sanctification and reconciliation of families, that they will come to know God’s healing love and mercy. Have mercy on the holy souls in purgatory, especially those whom we knew and loved, that they will soon share God’s glory in heaven. We pray that your faithful servants Father Baker, Father Seelos, Sister Lucy, Archbishop Fulton Sheen, Bishops Alfredo Obviar, Alfredo Verzosa, and Teofilo Camomot may soon be canonized. We beg you for the end of abortion, abuse of children and the elderly, for respect for the dignity of human life in all its stages, and please do not take your gaze off the unbaptized and missing children.

We thank you for all the blessings, known and unknown. We pour on you like water all our afflictions and sins for you to forgive and take away. Uproot everything in us that displeases you. May we go after your Heart and Mary’s and not exclude anyone from our love. Please give us our needs, known and unknown. You know them better than we do. We pray for the special intentions of everyone gathered here today and of those who cannot be with us. (Mention special intentions.)

May God grant us the grace to live our lives according in the mind and heart of Jesus so that we may always be in the divine presence and do God’s holy will.

These we ask in the name of your Son, Jesus. Amen.
